# Tone & Voice

How **Blocky World** talks — on the page, in menus, in toasts, and in the
README. Read this before writing or changing any user-facing text.

## Who we're talking to

Kids (and the grown-ups nearby), playing a tiny creative-mode sandbox in a
browser. They want to build stuff and have fun, not read a manual. Every line
should sound like a friendly person explaining a game to a curious eight-year-old
— never like a company explaining itself.

## The voice in one breath

Warm, plain, and concrete. Talk to the player directly, tell them what to do,
and use real things they can see and touch.

## Do

- **Talk to the player as "you."** _"Click an item to put it in your hand."_
- **Lead with a verb.** _"Punch trees for wood." "Tap the ground to dig."_
- **Stay concrete and physical.** Name the thing on screen — wood, an apple, a
  pickaxe, a furnace — not an abstraction.
- **Keep sentences short.** One idea per sentence. Read it out loud; if you run
  out of breath, cut it.
- **Be encouraging and gentle.** _"Eat apples so you don't starve!"_ _"Get
  water, go to the bathroom, stick your head outside."_
- **Use emoji with purpose**, the way the UI already does — to label or to add a
  little warmth (🌳 🍎 ⛏️), not to decorate every word.
- **Explain the why when it helps.** _"Batteries last much longer than coal."_

## Don't

- **No corporate or marketing voice.** No "we," no positioning, no trust claims,
  no talk of vendors, buyers, dashboards, or the market. This is a game, not a
  product pitch.
- **No abstract nouns standing in for real things** ("a neutral meter," "that
  position," "credibility"). If you can't point at it in the world, rewrite it.
- **No buzzwords or hype.** No "seamless," "powerful," "trusted," "best-in-class."
- **Don't lecture or over-explain.** If a button says what it does, let it.
- **Don't write for grown-up readers of fine print.** Write for the kid holding
  the phone.

## The smell test (a real "before")

This kind of copy is exactly what does **not** belong here:

> The same neutral meter every app and every buyer can trust. We govern spend,
> we never bill, and we never read prompts. No single vendor's own dashboard can
> credibly occupy that position.

It's abstract, it's about "us," and a kid can't see a single thing in it. If a
line sounds like that, delete it or rewrite it as something the player can do or
see.

## What "good" looks like (straight from the live site)

These already-shipped lines are the gold standard — match their rhythm:

- _"A tiny creative-mode sandbox."_
- _"Pick an item from the hotbar — it goes in your hand."_
- _"Point the + at a tree or 🍎 apple and tap to grab it."_
- _"Load a fuel (coal or a battery) and something to smelt, then press Smelt."_
- _"Villagers love emeralds — smelt emerald ore in a furnace to get them."_
- _"Get water, go to the bathroom, stick your head outside 🌳"_

## Quick checklist before you ship a line

1. Am I talking to **the player**, as "you"?
2. Does it start with or center on a **verb** — something to do?
3. Can the player **see or touch** everything I named?
4. Is it **short** enough to say in one breath?
5. Would it sound right to a **kid**, not a customer?
